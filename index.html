Why AI Matters

Artificial intelligence shapes the job market that students and workers in Maryland are entering right now. Tools like ChatGPT can debug code, draft emails, and summarize complex topics in seconds. Those benefits are real, but so is the pressure. Many of us wonder how long our current skills will stay relevant.

Research from Brookings shows that AI will transform tasks in about 0% of jobs across the United States, with some regions facing more disruption than others. Communities with weaker digital access are more vulnerable, especially when training opportunities are limited.

Strong Digital Access

  • More training options
  • Faster adaptation to new tools
  • Higher chances to move into tech-related roles

Limited Digital Access

  • Less training and support
  • Higher risk of job disruption
  • More stress and uncertainty

What the Research Says

Policy research from the White House and the European Commission shows that AI increases skill demands and widens gaps between high-skill and low-skill workers if training systems are weak. Other studies find that:

  • Workers feel more stress when AI enters their jobs without clear guidance or training.
  • Companies that invest in upskilling and reskilling see better performance and stability.
  • Communities with strong digital infrastructure experience smoother transitions.

Maryland Can Choose a Better Future

AI doesn‚Äôt have to destroy jobs. With planning, it can strengthen them. Here‚Äôs what Maryland should invest in:

1. Expand Digital Access

Invest in reliable internet and devices for underserved communities across the state.

2. Build AI & Data Training Programs
