About Me

My name is Angel G. Arroyo Buitrago, and I am a first-generation Hispanic computer science major at the University of Maryland. I grew up without consistent access to advanced technology at home, so I learned coding slowly, teaching myself line by line.

Those experiences shaped how I see artificial intelligence today. I use AI tools to test code, explore ideas, and learn faster,but I also see how easily people can be left behind when they donâ€™t have the same access, training, or support.

This blog grows out of a position paper I wrote for English 101 about AI, training, and the future of work. My goal is to move that academic argument into a public space where students, workers, and community members can think about what kind of AI-powered future Maryland should build.

What I Believe

  • AI should expand opportunity, not restrict it.
  • Every worker deserves fair access to training and digital tools.
  • First-generation and low-income students should not be left behind in AI transitions.
  • Governments, schools, and companies share responsibility for building fair systems.
